The Invisible Handshake — How APIs Replace Legal Contracts

Every API call is a micro‑contract. A POST is an offer. A 200 OK is acceptance. The internet’s economy runs not on paper agreements, but on promises executed in code.

You can’t sue a 502, but your SLA will.

1) Code as Contract: Offer, Acceptance, Consideration

  • Offer → Request: The client proposes a state change.
  • Acceptance → Response: The provider commits to an outcome.
  • Consideration → Data/Service: Value exchanged: records, money, or capability.

2) Trust Without Lawyers — The Protocol of Faith

APIs embed trust. Signatures attest authenticity, auth scopes limit power, idempotency prevents double‑spend, and audit logs preserve evidence. These are the terms of execution.

3) When Promises Break

Failure is not a legal dispute; it’s an engineering event. We contain blast radius, return consistent errors, and reconcile later. SLAs, quotas, and rate limits become the remedies.

4) The Future — Machine Law & Autonomous Commerce

As agents and smart contracts converge, APIs become the substrate of machine law: negotiable, verifiable, and auditable at runtime. The next legal revolution won’t happen in court — it’ll happen in the cloud.

Every POST is a promise. The handshake is invisible; the contract is code.
